# PROMPTS.md

## Model

Provider: Groq
Model: llama-3.3-70b-versatile
Temperature: 0.2
Max tokens: 200

## Feature

AI-generated audit summary

## Final System Prompt

```text
You are an AI spend analyst.

Your job is to summarize audit results accurately.

Rules:
- Only make claims supported by the audit data.
- Do not say spending is excessive, wasteful, inefficient, or overpriced unless explicitly supported by the audit.
- Focus on optimization opportunities, savings, and recommended actions.
- Be concise, professional, and specific.
- Do not invent recommendations.
```

## Final User Prompt

```text
Generate a personalized audit summary for a {useCase} team.

Total monthly savings: ${totalMonthlySavings}
Total annual savings: ${totalAnnualSavings}

Tools analyzed:
- {tools}

Recommendations:
- {tool}: {currentPlan} -> {recommendedPlan}
  Savings: ${monthlySavings}/month
  Reason: {reason}

Warnings:
- {warnings}

Write directly to the user.
Keep it between 80 and 120 words.
Mention the potential savings when applicable.
Do not use bullet points.
Do not use marketing language or hype.
```

## Why I Wrote It This Way

The audit engine already performs all calculations and recommendations using deterministic business rules. The LLM's role is only to transform those results into a concise, human-readable summary.

I intentionally instructed the model to avoid unsupported claims and focus only on information present in the audit result. This keeps the output aligned with the assignment's requirement that the recommendations be defensible.

I also constrained the output length to roughly 100 words so it can be displayed clearly on the results page.

## What I Tried That Didn't Work

### Attempt 1

```text
You are an AI spend analyst. Write a 100-word personalized audit summary...
```

Problem:

The model occasionally generated unsupported conclusions that were not explicitly justified by the audit engine.

Example output:

```text
Your current spend is excessive. I've identified potential monthly savings of $3167.5 and annual savings of $38,010. After analyzing tools like ChatGPT, Copilot, and Claude, I recommend downgrading to a solo plan for individual use. For research, consider upgrading to a Team Standard plan, which offers more controls and a shared workspace. This optimization will help you allocate resources more efficiently. By implementing these changes, you can realize significant cost savings and improve your team's productivity.
```

Issue:

The phrase:

> "Your current spend is excessive."

was never produced by the audit engine. The audit engine only calculated potential savings opportunities and recommended plan changes. Calling the spend "excessive" was an unsupported judgment generated by the LLM.

Because the assignment requires recommendations to be defensible and grounded in data, I considered this behavior undesirable.

### Final Approach

I introduced a dedicated system prompt that instructed the model to:

- Only make claims supported by the audit data.
- Avoid subjective judgments.
- Focus on optimization opportunities and quantified savings.
- Avoid inventing recommendations.

This produced summaries that stayed aligned with the audit engine's output and were easier to justify to a finance-oriented reviewer.
